Autism related to public service

People with autism are more likely to be victims of crimes and public service misinterpretation.

Posted by Charles Cusimano on April 27, 2023

Risks

Individuals with autism are at greater risk with wandering, abduction, child abuse, and general victimization because of their communication and behavioral deficits. This means that they need special attention from the first responders.

Public Services

Some actions by those with autism may be interpreted as criminal or dangerous to law enforcement. This is why it is important for law enforcement to be educated on autism and the behaviors that come with it, so that those with autism stay safe. A survey in New Jersey was sent out to all emergency services in the state in 2014. Out of all the participants, 46% of them said that the autism awareness training they received was only partially effective or not effective at all. 68% of participants reported that they had not gotten follow up training or a refresher on the two hour or less training they first received. This data shows that not enough time is spent on autism awareness in emergency services, and that the training is not effective enough. This puts in danger those with autism. Potentially thousands of officers who could respond to calls for autistic individuals have not been properly trained to do so. New methods need to be put in place to ensure the safety of autistic individuals. The training on autism awareness needs to be improved not just in the state of New Jersey, but nationwide.
